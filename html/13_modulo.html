Modulo
Unlike JS, in Python, % takes the sign of Divisor!
Python uses floor division to calculate the modulo, according to the following formula
$$ remainder = dividend - (divisor * floor(\frac{dividend}{divisor})) $$
Other languages, such as JS, use the following formula, where trunc is truncated division which always rounds towards zero
$$ remainder = dividend - (divisor *trunc(\frac{dividend}{divisor})) $$

For Negative Divisor OR Negative Dividend

  1. Calculate the remainder by following formula

    $$ remainder = | |dividend| - (|divisor| * ceiling(\frac{|dividend|}{|divisor|})) | $$
  2. Replace sign with sign of the divisor
If both divisor and dividend are negative, calculate using absolute values, and make sign of remainder equal to divisor

Examples
Example 1
Negative divisor (worked example)
for 10 % (-3)
$$ remainder = 10- ((-3) * floor(\frac{10}{(-3)})) $$ $$ remainder = 10- ((-3) * (-4)) $$ $$ remainder = 10-12 = -2 $$
Example 2 
    10  %  (-3)  //  -2
   -10  %    3   //   2
    10  %   -3   //  -2
Example 3 (Division by zero)

Example 4 
    33  % -12   //  -3
   -33  %  12   //   3
    33  %  12   //   9
   -33  % -12   //  -9
Notes
Dividend/Divisor

20 / 5 = 4

What is being divided is called the dividend,

which is divided by the divisor,

and the result is called the quotient

In the example, 20 is the dividend, 5 is the divisor, and 4 is the quotient.

Additional Formula
N = D * ( N // D) + (N % D)
